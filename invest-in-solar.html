Good to know

Investor questions

What size of investment are we talking about?
A 1–2 MW plant is a multi-crore investment. The exact figure depends on land, model, and site. We'll prepare detailed project economics for your specific case on a consultation, including projected returns and payback.
How predictable are the returns?
Solar generation is highly predictable over a year, and with contracted off-take (open access or group captive) your revenue is largely locked in. Returns of 18–25% are typical, but we'll model your specific project rather than rely on averages.
What about the tax benefits?
Accelerated depreciation allows you to write down a large share of the asset value in the early years, reducing taxable income. This materially improves your effective return. We'll walk through it with figures for your situation.
Do I have to manage the plant?
No. Our O&M team runs it, handling monitoring, maintenance, and performance optimisation, so it stays a passive investment for you.
How much land does an MW-scale plant need?
As a rough guide, a megawatt of ground-mounted solar needs around 4–5 acres, depending on terrain and layout. We assess land you already hold or help identify suitable sites, and factor it into the project economics.
What secures the income from the plant?
Revenue is anchored by a long-term off-take arrangement, such as open access or group captive, where a creditworthy consumer contracts to buy the power. We structure the off-take and assess counterparty strength so the cash flow is as secure as the asset.
How do we get started, and how long until it's running?
It starts with a consultation where we model your specific project economics. From approvals to commissioning, an MW-scale plant typically takes several months, which we manage end to end, including the regulatory and grid approvals.
